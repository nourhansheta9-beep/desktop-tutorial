# Payer & Funding Playbook

**The single biggest lever to 2–3× volume is payer access + approval throughput — not more cold calls.**
Every unit you sell is gated by "who pays and how fast it gets approved." Today you lean on ADP/ODSP/March-of-Dimes. There are at least **6 more funded channels** you can switch on, several within weeks.

> **Compliance first (non-negotiable):** "Maximize usage" means find **every client who legitimately qualifies** and **every program that legitimately covers them** — not over-billing. ADP, WSIB, VAC, NIHB and insurers all audit. Bill only eligible clients, with proper authorizer assessment, correct device codes, real medical necessity, and a prescription where required. Never pay referral sources for steering, and never waive co-pays as an inducement. Protect patient choice. Done right, this is huge and durable. Done wrong, it ends in deregistration and prosecution. See `org-and-team-design.md` for the Funding & Intake desk that owns this correctly.

## Tier 0 — Fast unlocks (start this week, revenue inside H2)

These need little or no long lead time and open **new addressable demand immediately**.

### 1. Private insurance via Assignment of Benefits (AOB) — *highest-ROI quick win*
- Carriers that accept AOB include **Manulife, Sun Life, Canada Life, GreenShield**, plus Desjardins, Beneva, iA, Empire, Equitable, Blue Cross. With AOB you **bill the insurer directly** and the client pays only the gap.
- Group plans frequently cover **DME at ~100% up to ~$5,000–$10,000/year**. Items over ~$500 usually need **predetermination** (pre-approval) + prescription + proof of medical necessity.
- **Action:** stand up an AOB billing process; train staff to **verify benefits before quoting** and submit predeterminations. This converts "too expensive" objections into "covered." 
- **Owner:** Funding & Intake desk + RCM. **KPI:** % of quotes with verified insurance, AOB claims submitted/week.
- Sources: [Durable medical equipment coverage via Canadian health plans](https://healthquotes.ca/durable-medical-equipment-coverage-via-canadian-health-insurance/), [Sun Life personal health plans](https://www.sunlife.ca/en/health/personal-health-insurance/enhanced-plan/).

### 2. WSIB (workplace injuries)
- Covers assistive devices, mobility and home mods tied to an approved claim, **100%**, with **direct billing** once approved.
- High-value, fast-pay, often complex/bariatric rehab — underused by most HME shops.
- **Action:** enroll as a WSIB provider; build a referral line to WSIB case managers and physiatrists.
- Source: [WSIB / funding overview](https://finalmedic.ca/disability-equipment-funding-canada-guide/).

### 3. Veterans Affairs Canada (VAC) — administered by **Medavie Blue Cross**
- **100%** coverage for eligible veterans: mobility aids, bathroom safety, CPAP, ostomy/catheter, hearing, prosthetics. Loyal, high-retention, recurring-supply clients.
- **Action:** register as a provider with Medavie Blue Cross; market to Legions and veteran networks.

### 4. ODSP top-up on existing ADP pipeline
- For **ODSP/OW/ACSD** recipients, **ADP pays 100%** (not 75%), and ODSP can cover supplies/repairs/batteries via **Mandatory Special Necessities**.
- **Action:** screen every ADP client for ODSP status and convert the 25% co-pay to 100% funded. Pure margin capture on deals you already have.

### 5. Service-sales reactivation (no new payer needed)
- Your owned customer base is a goldmine: warranty expiries, aging equipment, **ADP re-eligibility cycles** (e.g., many mobility devices ~every 5 years), resupply due dates.
- **Action:** Mohamed Hassany's service-sales team works the existing database with a call cadence. See recurring revenue below.

## Tier 1 — Channel builds (start now, land mid-to-late H2)

### 6. Hospital-at-Home / Hospital-to-Home (H2H)
- Ontario is putting **$1.1B over 3 years** into home care and H2H; the program supported **~8,100 patients across 47 sites in 2024–25** and is expanding (Ottawa Hospital, Windsor Regional, etc.).
- Acute-care-at-home needs **rental** equipment fast: beds, oxygen, mobility, monitoring, safety.
- **Wedge:** a **rental fleet** + a **24–48h delivery/setup SLA**. Your 5-person delivery team is a real asset here.
- **Action:** map local H2H sites; get on their discharge/equipment lists; pitch reliability + speed (not inducements).
- Sources: [Ontario invests $1.1B to expand home care / H2H](https://www.homecarepost.com/ontario-invests-1-1-billion-to-expand-home-care-hospital-to-home-programs/), [The Ottawa Hospital launches Hospital at Home](https://www.ottawahospital.on.ca/en/newsroom/the-ottawa-hospital-launches-hospital-at-home-to-expand-access-to-acute-care-in-the-community/).

### 7. NIHB (First Nations & Inuit)
- **100%, no annual cap** when prescribed; direct billing. Needs Rx + letter of medical necessity + quote + prior approval over the standard rate.
- **Action:** become an NIHB-registered vendor; build relationships with relevant health centres.

### 8. March of Dimes HVMP + Ontario Renovates (home/vehicle mods)
- HVMP: **home mods lifetime $15k**, **vehicle mods $15k/10yr**, for Ontario residents with household income **< $60k** and a 1+ year mobility disability. Funded by the Ministry for Seniors and Accessibility.
- **Action:** if you do/partner on ramps, stairlifts, roll-in showers, bath safety — help clients apply, and stack with municipal **Ontario Renovates**. This is an adjacent, higher-ticket line.
- Source: [March of Dimes Home & Vehicle Modification Program](https://www.marchofdimes.ca/en-ca/programs/am/hvmp).

## Tier 2 — Strategic / longer lead time (start the paperwork now)

### 9. Expand ADP device categories you're registered for
- ADP covers **8,000+ items** across mobility/seating, **respiratory, home oxygen**, prosthetics, orthotics, communication, enteral feeding, etc. Each category you add expands what you can fund at 75–100%.
- **Reality check on timing:** ADP's target to review a **new vendor/category registration is ~6 months** from a complete application. So categories started now mostly pay off **late H2 / H1 next year** — still worth starting immediately.
- **Action:** read the [Vendor Registration Guide](https://www.ontario.ca/files/2025-04/moh-adp-vendor-registration-guide-en-2025-04-30.pdf) and [register as a vendor](https://www.health.gov.on.ca/en/pro/programs/adp/vendor_registration.aspx) for high-value categories you're missing (respiratory/oxygen is recurring-revenue gold). Contact: adp@ontario.ca / 1-800-268-6021.

### 10. Ontario Health atHome — provincial formulary & prequalification
- The provincial home-care body contracts equipment vendors **regionally** (incumbents: **Medigas, Ontario Medical Supply, Rexall**) and runs a **prequalification → procurement** process plus a **Provincial Formulary** for equipment and supplies.
- **Action:** pursue **prequalification** and **formulary listing**, or **subcontract** to an incumbent in regions you can serve well. Join **OHMEPA** (Ontario Home Medical Equipment Providers Association) for visibility and intel.
- Sources: [Ontario Health prequalification](https://www.ontariohealth.ca/clinical/home-community/prequalification), [Provincial Formulary – Medical Equipment](https://ontariohealthathome.ca/document/provincial-formulary-medical-equipment/), [OHMEPA](https://ohmepa.com/).

## Recurring revenue: the compounding engine

One-time sales don't triple a business; **recurring** does. Build these now:

- **Resupply programs** — CPAP masks/filters, oxygen, enteral, incontinence, ostomy on auto-cadence. (Requires the relevant ADP/insurer registrations.) This is the proven recurring-revenue stream in HME.
- **Rental fleet** — beds, oxygen concentrators, wheelchairs, lifts for **short-term/acute (Hospital-at-Home)** and retirement homes. Track rental caps (ownership transfer) and utilization.
- **Preventive-maintenance (PM) service contracts** — annual contracts with retirement/LTC homes and your installed base; bill repairs to ADP/insurer where eligible. This is where your technician capacity becomes a revenue center, not a cost.

Source: [DME recurring revenue / resupply & rental models](https://nikohealth.com/how-to-start-a-durable-medical-equipment-business-the-ultimate-guide/).

## Funding-desk operating rhythm (how throughput actually goes up)

1. **Benefit triage at first contact** — every lead is screened for *all* payers (ADP, ODSP, insurance, WSIB, VAC, NIHB, MOD) before quoting. One intake form, every program.
2. **Authorizer network** — a managed roster of registered **OTs/PTs/physicians** who can assess and authorize quickly. *Approvals are gated by authorizer availability — this is often the real bottleneck, not demand.*
3. **Predetermination factory** — standardized templates for insurance predeterminations, NIHB LMNs, WSIB submissions; track cycle time and approval rate.
4. **Denial management** — log every denial reason, fix, resubmit. Denials are recoverable revenue.

> Track these as KPIs: **approval cycle time**, **approval rate**, **% pipeline with verified funding**, **revenue by payer**. If you only add one capability this year, make it this desk.
