# Market & Channel Strategy

## The tailwind is real and it's now

- Ontario's **65+ population is growing ~2.2%/year**; the GTA's 65+ cohort is projected to **nearly double by 2041**. Mobility aids are already the **largest share** of Ontario medical-equipment purchases, with broad ADP eligibility.
- The province is deliberately **shifting care into the home**: **$1.1B more** into home care and Hospital-to-Home, which supported **~8,100 patients across 47 sites** in 2024–25 and is expanding.
- LTC bed targets (58,000 new beds by 2028) are **behind schedule**, which pushes *more* demand into home and retirement settings — exactly where you operate.

Translation: demand is not the constraint. **Access (payers + referral relationships + institutional contracts) is.** This plan attacks access on five fronts.

Sources: [Ontario aging/market](https://www.factorydirectmedical.com/blogs/news/ontario-medical-supply), [$1.1B home care / H2H](https://www.homecarepost.com/ontario-invests-1-1-billion-to-expand-home-care-hospital-to-home-programs/), [CBC: LTC bed shortfall](https://www.cbc.ca/news/canada/toronto/ontario-budget-healthcare-long-term-care-9.7143637).

## Front 1 — Hospitals & Hospital-at-Home: become the obvious choice (compliantly)

**Goal:** be the equipment partner discharge teams reach for — won on **reliability and speed**, not inducements.

> **Compliance guardrail (read this):** Steering referrals to a "preferred" vendor *without protecting patient choice* is treated as **suspect**, and paying referrers is prohibited. You win this channel legitimately: by being on **formularies/approved lists**, hitting **SLAs**, and making discharge planners' lives easier — never by paying for referrals or waiving co-pays as a kickback. ([discharge-planning/DME compliance](https://www.homecaremag.com/mag/medical_dealing_hospital_discharge))

**How you actually win it:**
1. **Rapid-fulfillment SLA** — commit to 24–48h (or same-day for acute) delivery + setup. Your 5-person delivery team is the differentiator most competitors can't match.
2. **Rental fleet for acute-at-home** — beds, oxygen, mobility, monitoring, safety. Hospital-at-Home needs *short-term rentals*, fast. Stand this up (see funding playbook → recurring revenue).
3. **Get on the lists** — hospital procurement / equipment formularies, and the **Ontario Health atHome provincial formulary**. Map the local Hospital-at-Home sites and introduce yourself to discharge planning and the OT/PT teams.
4. **Make discharge easy** — a single intake form, on-the-spot funding triage (ADP/insurance/WSIB/VAC), and "we handle the paperwork" is what gets you called first.
5. **Clinical credibility** — your Community Liaison runs lunch-and-learns and is reachable; reliability + responsiveness compounds into being the default.

**Owner:** Mohamed Alyoussef (Institutional BD). **KPIs:** hospital/H2H sites active, rental units deployed, SLA hit-rate, referrals/site/month.

## Front 2 — Ontario Health atHome (the big public channel)

- The provincial home-care body contracts equipment vendors **regionally** (incumbents **Medigas, Ontario Medical Supply, Rexall**), runs a **prequalification → procurement** process, and maintains a **Provincial Formulary**.
- **Plays:** (a) pursue **prequalification + formulary listing**; (b) where you can't win the prime contract yet, **subcontract** to an incumbent in regions you serve well; (c) join **OHMEPA** for intel and visibility.
- **Reality:** longer lead time (procurement cycles), so **start now** but don't budget H2 revenue on it. Treat it as the H1-next-year prize you seed this half.
- **Owner:** Saeed + Mohamed Alyoussef. Sources: [prequalification](https://www.ontariohealth.ca/clinical/home-community/prequalification), [partners/vendors](https://ontariohealthathome.ca/partners/).

## Front 3 — Retirement homes & LTC (your existing B2B, scaled)

- **ORCA (Ontario Retirement Communities Association)** represents **~92% of licensed retirement suites** (~60,000 seniors) and has **240+ commercial partners** supplying products/services. **AdvantAge Ontario** represents **500+ not-for-profit** seniors'-care members; **OLTCA** the for-profit LTC side.
- **Plays:**
  1. **Become an ORCA commercial partner** (and engage AdvantAge/OLTCA) — direct line to the buyers.
  2. **Chain-level preferred-supplier agreements** — standardize beds/mobility/bath-safety across a chain's homes (bulk + replacement cycles).
  3. **On-site assessment/clinic days** — sell to residents (private-pay + ADP) where they live; the home gets a service, you get qualified buyers.
  4. **PM service contracts** — annual maintenance on each home's equipment fleet (recurring revenue + your technician team becomes a profit center).
- **Owner:** Mohamed Alyoussef + Moayed. Sources: [ORCA](https://www.orcaretirement.com/), [AdvantAge Ontario](https://www.advantageontario.ca/).

## Front 4 — Referral engine (OT / PT / physicians / discharge planners)

- DME demand is **prescriber- and OT-driven**: a physician signs the order; the **OT** assesses mobility/ADL and is often the one who reduces readmissions and specifies the equipment.
- **Plays:** a managed list of referral sources per territory; a fast, friendly intake; "we do the funding paperwork"; quarterly education sessions. Track **referrals by source** in the CRM so you double down on what works.
- **Owner:** Community Liaison (new) → feeds Nourhan (inbound) and the field team. Source: [OT role in discharge & readmission reduction](https://healthbound.ca/hospital-discharge-planning-with-an-occupational-therapist/).

## Positioning vs. the field

Competitors include big regionals (e.g., **Motion**, **Shoppers Home Health Care**, **Silver Cross/Medichair**) and the Ontario Health atHome incumbents (**OMS, Medigas, Rexall**). You won't out-scale them on day one — you out-**serve** them:

- **Speed & setup** — same/next-day delivery and in-home setup that big players are slow at.
- **Funding done-for-you** — clients and discharge planners pick whoever removes the paperwork pain.
- **Local, multilingual, relationship-driven** — your field/B2B team's coverage and responsiveness.
- **Recurring care** — resupply + PM contracts keep you in the account, not just a one-time sale.

> Pick a **beachhead**: 2–3 local hospitals/H2H sites + 1–2 retirement chains + the fast payer unlocks. Win those decisively in H2, then expand region by region.
