*Written as if this were my company. This is the master operating plan; each department has its own playbook (`01`–`08`) with structure, every person's role, targets, income lines, comp, and cadence.*

---

## 1. North star

**Be the GTA's fastest, most funding-savvy mobility & home-accessibility partner — so that hospitals, retirement homes, OTs, and families call us first.**

Financial goal: take the **measured streams from ~$100k/mo to ~$200k/mo (2×) by December**, with a path to **~$300k/mo (3×)** — *and* grow the **Field acquisition + Institutional/B2B engines that sit on top of that base*. The whole company can comfortably outgrow 2× because B2B and outside sales aren't even in the $100k.

How we win, in one sentence: **we remove the two things that slow this industry down — funding paperwork and slow delivery — and we never let a qualified lead die.**

## 6. Compensation philosophy — pay for what compounds

- **Base + variable** for every revenue role. Variable pays on **approved/collected** revenue (not quoted) — aligns sales with the Funding Desk and protects cash.
- **Reward recurring over one-time:** higher commission rate on PM contracts, resupply subscriptions, and rentals than on one-off sales.
- **Funding Desk bonus** on *throughput*: approval rate, cycle-time, and $ unlocked — so the desk pushes volume, not just files paper.
- **Delivery/Technical spiffs** for upsell flags and PM attach — your drivers and techs are in 100+ homes/month; pay them to spot reorders and safety upgrades.
- **Company-wide bonus** when the monthly run-rate hits each milestone ($130k → $160k → $200k). Everyone rows together.
- Detailed structures live in each department file.

## 8. Risks & guardrails

- **Compliance is the licence to operate.** Ethical funding maximization only; proper authorizer assessment; medical necessity; no inducements; protect patient choice; PHIPA on all data. (ADP/WSIB/insurers audit — the downside is deregistration.)
- **Cash/AR:** direct-bill wherever possible; watch AR days; finance the gap for clients so price never kills a deal — but don't let receivables balloon.
- **Capacity gating:** the revenue won't appear without the hires in §3 — don't set targets ahead of capacity.
- **Key-person risk:** document SOPs (the templates are a start); cross-train; don't let any single person be a single point of failure (esp. Funding and Technical).
