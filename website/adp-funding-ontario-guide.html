A plain-language guide to ADP funding in Ontario

6 min read · Funding

Ontario’s Assistive Devices Program (ADP) helps pay for mobility and other equipment — but the rules can feel confusing. Here’s the simple version.

What is ADP?

The Assistive Devices Program is a Government of Ontario program that helps people with long-term physical disabilities pay for personal equipment like wheelchairs, scooters, walkers, and specialized seating. It’s designed to make essential mobility equipment affordable.

How much does ADP pay?

For most mobility equipment, ADP pays up to 75% of the approved amount. If you receive Ontario Disability Support Program (ODSP) or Ontario Works (OW) benefits, ADP typically covers 100%.

The remaining 25% can often be covered by private insurance or, for eligible clients, by ODSP. We help you put the pieces together so your out-of-pocket cost is as low as possible.

Who qualifies?

  • You’re an Ontario resident with a valid health card
  • You have a long-term physical disability (6 months or longer)
  • You’ve been assessed by a registered authorizer (such as an OT or PT)

How do you apply?

Get assessed

A registered authorizer evaluates your needs and recommends equipment.

Choose a vendor

Equipment must be supplied by an ADP-authorized vendor — like Help Mobility.

Submit & approve

The paperwork goes to ADP for approval.

Receive your equipment

Once approved, we deliver, set up, and bill ADP directly for its portion.

How Help Mobility helps

As an ADP-authorized vendor, we arrange the assessment, prepare the paperwork correctly the first time, and coordinate any additional funding — so you can focus on getting mobile, not on forms.

This article is general information, not medical or funding advice. Program rules change and every situation is different — contact us to confirm what applies to you.
