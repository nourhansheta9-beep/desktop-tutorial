Questions, answered

Funding FAQ

What is ADP and what does it cover?

The Assistive Devices Program (ADP) is an Ontario government program that helps pay for personal mobility and other equipment for people with long-term physical disabilities. It covers wheelchairs, scooters, seating, walkers and many other devices. ADP typically pays up to 75% of the approved amount — and 100% for people receiving ODSP or Ontario Works.

How do I qualify for ADP funding?

You generally need to be an Ontario resident with a valid health card and a long-term (6 months or more) physical disability, and be assessed by a registered authorizer (such as an Occupational Therapist or Physiotherapist). We help arrange that assessment and handle the paperwork from there.

Do I have to pay anything up front?

It depends on your funding mix. With ADP, you’re typically responsible for the 25% portion (often covered if you’re on ODSP/OW, or claimable through private insurance). Where we can bill insurers directly through Assignment of Benefits, you pay only the remaining gap. We always confirm your costs before you commit.

Can I use more than one funding source?

Yes — funding often stacks. For example, ADP can cover the bulk of a wheelchair while private insurance covers your co-pay, or March of Dimes funds a ramp while insurance covers bath safety. We map out the best combination for your situation.

How long does approval take?

It varies by program and how quickly an assessment can be scheduled. Some private-insurance predeterminations come back in days; ADP and program approvals can take longer. We move things along by preparing complete, accurate submissions the first time.

What if I don’t qualify for any program?

We’ll tell you honestly, and offer fair self-pay pricing plus financing options so cost doesn’t stand between you and staying mobile. Renting is also an affordable alternative for short-term needs.

Answers are general guidance. Program rules change — we confirm current details for your specific situation.
