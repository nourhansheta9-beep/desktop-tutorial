How to choose the right mobility aid

5 min read · Buying guide

Cane, walker, rollator, scooter, or wheelchair? The right choice depends on your strength, balance, home, and how far you travel. Here’s how to think it through.

Start with how you move today

The best mobility aid supports you where you need it without doing too much for you (which can reduce strength over time). An honest look at your balance, endurance, and daily routine is the best starting point — and an Occupational Therapist can assess this precisely.

A quick comparison

AidBest for
CaneMild balance support, one-sided weakness
WalkerSteady support, indoor use
RollatorWalking longer distances, needs a seat to rest
ScooterLimited stamina, travels longer distances outdoors
WheelchairCannot walk safely or for long; full-time mobility

Think about your home

  • Doorway widths and turning space
  • Stairs or thresholds (you may also need a ramp or stairlift)
  • Where the equipment will be stored and charged
  • Whether you’ll transport it in a vehicle

Don’t forget funding

Many of these are ADP- or insurance-funded. Choosing a funded option that fits is often more affordable than people expect — we’ll check what you qualify for before you decide.
